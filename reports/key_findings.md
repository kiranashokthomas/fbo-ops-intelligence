# Key Findings - FBO Operations Intelligence Dashboard

## Summary

All findings are derived from FAA ATADS airport operations data (2024-2025) and FAA aviation facilities metadata for TEB, HPN, and PWK.

---

## Finding 1: TEB - Structural High-Pressure FBO Location

- FBO Exposure Ratio: ~100% - virtually all operations are air taxi or general aviation.
- Highest FBO Workload Score of all three airports in 2024 and 2025.
- Total operations YoY: -0.96% but absolute workload remains highest.
- Operational meaning: An FBO at TEB must maintain full staffing, fueling readiness, and ramp management at all times.

## Finding 2: HPN - Growth-Pressure FBO Location

- Air taxi YoY growth: +13.67% - fastest growing FBO-relevant segment.
- General aviation YoY growth: +1.20%
- Total operations YoY growth: +4.24%
- Operational meaning: HPN is the growth market. Proactive capacity planning is critical.

## Finding 3: PWK - Stable FBO Environment

- Total operations YoY growth: +2.44% - moderate and consistent.
- Lower sensitivity to air taxi fluctuations than TEB and HPN.
- Operational meaning: Focus on service quality and consistency rather than reactive surge capacity.

## Finding 4: TEB Most Sensitive to Air Taxi Surges

- A simulated +10% increase in air taxi movements produces the largest workload score jump at TEB.
- FBOs at TEB need dynamic staffing models and real-time resource allocation for short-notice surges.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. TEB: Prioritise operational agility and staffing depth. Pre-position fuel trucks and invest in ramp management tooling.
2. HPN: Plan for capacity expansion now. Review fuel storage, ramp staffing, and lounge capacity for near-term investment.
3. PWK: Maintain service consistency. Stable environment is an asset - focus on turnaround time metrics.
